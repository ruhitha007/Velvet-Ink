What We Wear When No One Is Watching

There is a version of ourselves that only exists in private — the self we dress for when the only audience is the mirror. This piece explores the radical honesty of solitary fashion: the garments we choose not to be seen in, and what they reveal about who we truly are beneath the performance.

Fashion historians often note that the most revealing clothes in history were those worn in private. The corset a woman loosened the moment her husband left. The bright color a widow wore only in her garden. The man's jacket that lived at the bottom of a trunk until its owner could finally wear it openly.
